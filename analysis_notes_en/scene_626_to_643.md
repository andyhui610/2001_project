# Audiovisual Analysis of Shots #626 to #643: The Monolith's Bedside Descent, the Star Child's Rebirth, and the Ultimate Evolutionary Loop of *Also sprach Zarathustra*

This analysis note integrates shots #626 to #643 into a grouped study. Spanning approximately 4 minutes and 28 seconds (longer if the post-credits black screen is included), this sequence constitutes the ultimate climax and philosophical destination of *2001: A Space Odyssey*. In this segment, the dying, elderly Bowman tremblingly raises his right hand, pointing toward the bedside where the colossal Monolith has materialized out of thin air. Subsequently, his physical body perishes within a pitch-black dimensional channel, transforming into the "Star Child"—a luminous embryo floating in space, gazing down upon Earth with wide, open eyes. Through the visual motif of Michelangelo's "Genesis finger," the absolute blackness of the Monolith dissolving space, the counterpoint geometry of the Star Child and Earth, and the epic recurrence of Richard Strauss's *Also sprach Zarathustra*, Kubrick accomplishes a magnificent narrative, visual, and musical circle, rendering the ultimate transcendence of human evolution as a nirvana-like baptism combining cosmic religiosity and existential contemplation.

---

## 1. Composition & Cinematography

* **Visual Intertextuality with "The Creation of Adam" & The Evolutionary Threshold Hand**:
  * As the dying Bowman lies on the classically decorated bed, the camera employs a medium close-up to capture him using the last ounce of his physical strength to slowly lift his withered, liver-spotted right hand, his index finger pointing forward.
  * Visually, this gesture directly references the finger of Adam reaching toward God to receive the spark of life in Michelangelo's Sistine Chapel fresco, *The Creation of Adam*.
  * Here, Kubrick executes a stroke of science fiction genius: the "God" deep in the frame is replaced by the solemnly towering, silent Monolith. This trembling finger pointing toward the Monolith becomes humanity's final yearning and reach for higher cosmic intelligence and ultimate truth prior to physical expiration, symbolizing the formal transfer of a new epoch of evolutionary grace.
* **Monolithic Symmetry & Spatial Halving**:
  * In Bowman's subjective perspective, the Monolith stands vertically at the foot of the bed. The Monolith's absolute geometric ratio (1:4:9) aligns perfectly with the classical symmetrical walls and the grid of the luminous floor in the Baroque room, dividing the entire space along the central axis into two equal halves.
  * The absolute verticality and rigid lines of the Monolith form an extreme visual contrast with the withered, curved contours of the dying old man on his deathbed. Subsequently, the camera tracks in slowly and resolutely along the central axis toward the Monolith. This movement is not just a physical approach through space, but a plunge of subjective consciousness into the "higher-dimensional abyss" represented by the Monolith. Once pure blackness completely fills the frame, the three-dimensional spatial geometry of the Baroque room is thoroughly dismantled.
* **Star Child vs. Earth Circular Geometry & Cosmic Gaze**:
  * As the frame brightens again from the absolute blackness of the Monolith, the Baroque room has completely vanished, replaced by the vastness of deep space. Gestating inside a luminous blue-white embryonic sac, the Star Child appears on the left side of the screen in a slight profile, his eyes wide open, calmly gazing ahead.
  * On the right side of the frame is the circular, azure-glowing Earth. The round contour of the Star Child's embryo and the circular celestial geometry of Earth form a perfect circular counterpoint (Match Circular Geometry) on screen.
  * This geometric isomorphism between celestial body and embryo visually merges the "microscopic nova of life" with the "macroscopic planetary mother," indicating that humanity has transcended the limitations of individual and tool, returning to the cradle of the cosmos to initiate the endless gaze of a brand-new evolutionary cycle.

## 2. Color & Lighting Aesthetics

* **The Monolithic Void & Dimensional Rupture**:
  * Standing upright at the foot of the bed, the Monolith presents a matte, light-absorbing "absolute blackness." Despite the intense diffused white light from the floor panels below, the Monolith's surface exhibits no highlights and casts no shadow onto the ground.
  * This lighting design shatters the optical balance of the Baroque room. On screen, the Monolith does not look like a three-dimensional object, but rather a "visual void" or a "spatial patch" clipped out of the three-dimensional world. In terms of color and lighting, this externalizes the relentless invasion and dimensional transcendence of higher-dimensional existence into the three-dimensional human simulated environment.
* **Embryonic Blue-White Glow & Transcendental Luminescence**:
  * Distinct from the uniform, cold, and dehumanized light of the luminous floor in the Baroque room, the embryonic sac housing the Star Child radiates a warm, sacred blue-white transcendental luminescence.
  * This glow possesses a liquid clarity and the warmth of organic life, enveloping the pure, flawless face of the Star Child. Here, light and shadow achieve a qualitative leap from the "sterile illumination of scientific instruments" to "sacred spiritual self-luminescence," symbolizing that after enduring the darkness of physical death, Bowman completes a brilliant rebirth of nirvana on a spiritual level.
* **Cosmic Navy Blue & Return to the Womb**:
  * The deep space at the film's conclusion is not the chaotic multicolored radiation of the Stargate corridor, but a return to a deep, tranquil blue-black with a sacred velvet texture (Cosmic Navy Blue).
  * The azure Earth in the background casts a soft, peaceful blue halo, mirroring the glow of the Star Child's embryo. The dominance of blue and blue-black visually establishes an extremely serene sense of a "return to the womb." At this moment, the vastness of deep space is no longer the icy, life-repelling vacuum of Chapters 1 and 2; instead, it is redefined as a warm, inclusive "Cosmic Womb" gestating extraordinary life.

## 3. Sound & Music Design

* **The Sacred Silence at the Threshold**:
  * Throughout the entire deathbed threshold as the elderly Bowman points toward the foot of the bed and the Monolith towers, the soundtrack undergoes the most prolonged, breathtaking silence in the film. The faint physical breathing sound from the previous segment is completely drained away, plunging the audio into a state of unadulterated vacuum.
  * This "Sacred Silence" auditorily constructs a powerful sense of ritual and tension. It forces the audience to hold their breath alongside the dying man, confronting the termination of physical life and completely relinquishing auditory focus to the visual presence of the Monolith, perfectly building dynamic energy for the orchestral eruption that follows.
* **Also sprach Zarathustra & The Musical Ring-Structure**:
  * As the Star Child is born in intense light and the shot cuts to the new life floating in space, the silent soundtrack is shattered by the overwhelming orchestra of *Also sprach Zarathustra*. The magnificent brass, the low hum of the organ, and the heavy strikes of the timpani sweep through the theater with peerless power.
  * This is the third and final appearance of the piece in the film, completing a sacred loop in the film's musical design. These three occurrences correspond to three pivotal leaps in human evolution:
    1. **The Sunrise at the Beginning**: The birth of celestial bodies and the material world (the starting point of physical evolution).
    2. **The Dawn of Man Bone-Smashing**: The discovery of tools and the birth of "man" (the starting point of technological evolution).
    3. **The Star Child Floating in Space Ending**: The transcendence of the physical body and the birth of the "Overman" (the starting point of spiritual and higher-dimensional evolution).
  * The recurrence of the music elevates this long space exploration into a sacred epic of cosmic evolution; every beat of the timpani acts as the pulse of a new life, shaking the viewer's soul.
* **Timpani Crescendo & Cut to Black Silence**:
  * As the thematic melody of *Also sprach Zarathustra* ascends to its final grand chord, the timpani delivers its final strikes at fortissimo. Just as the reverberation of the last drumbeat vibrates in the air, the frame abruptly hard-cuts to black, transitioning into the silent rolling credits.
  * This sound-editing method of "forte-abrupt stop-black screen" leaves a prolonged and deep acoustic resonance in the listener's mind. Rejecting the warm and gentle curtain calls typical of conventional Hollywood movies, it uses a powerful sonic exclamation point to permanently freeze the Star Child's gaze and the musical shock in the depths of the audience's consciousness.

## 4. Editing & Script Adaptation

* **Symbolic Montage & Metaphysical Leap**:
  * Kubrick abandons all causal narrative relationships of conventional drama here. The editing leaps solemnly from "finger pointing ➡️ Monolith standing ➡️ track-in on pure blackness ➡️ Star Child in space."
  * This "Symbolic Montage" makes no attempt to explain "how the physical body died" or "where the embryo originated." Instead, it uses visual collage to translate highly abstract existentialist and evolutionary philosophy concepts directly into pure visual transitions. Editing here is no longer for the purpose of storytelling; it becomes an alchemy catalyzing the qualitative transformation of Bowman's consciousness, completing the ultimate transcendence of cinematic image over written language.
* **De-politicization & The Sublime Philosophical Mystery**:
  * **Original Script and Novel Configurations**: In Arthur C. Clarke's original screenplay and novel, the birth of the Star Child was accompanied by highly concrete action descriptions. Clarke wrote that the Star Child returned to Earth at the speed of thought, discovered nuclear weapon satellites deployed in orbit by the Cold War superpowers (the US and USSR), and detonated these bombs using telekinesis, saving humanity on the brink of nuclear destruction.
  * **Kubrick's Genius Re-creation**: In the final cut, Kubrick completely deleted these concrete sci-fi actions carrying Cold War backgrounds and geopolitical undertones. At the film's end, the Star Child simply floats silently in deep space, eyes wide and deep, gazing calmly and transcendentally at the Earth, before cutting to black.
  * This major screenplay adaptation was key to cementing the film's status as a masterpiece in cinema history. By deleting the specific plot of "saving the Earth," Kubrick elevated the philosophical meaning of the ending from an "American savior sci-fi story" into a magnificent, unsolved mystery about "the cycle of life, the eternity of consciousness, and cosmic nirvana," returning the ultimate right of interpretation completely to the audience, giving the film an eternal artistic tension.
